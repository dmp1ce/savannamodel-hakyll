--- book: Deadly Harvest page: 66 tags: cheese,milk --- . . . Slavs, Germans, Scandinavians, and Anglo-Saxons became dairy farmers . . . , focusing on the cow. Some parts of southern Europe adopted, in a minor way, sheep's milk and goat's milk. Roquefort cheese is made from sheep's milk in Toulouse, France, and the Greeks use goat's milk to make feta cheese. To the east, the Mongols took up the practice of dairying with the yak (a kind of massive ox).
